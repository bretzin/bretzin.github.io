Corporate Structure

Four Business Lines. One Closed Loop.

BKV operates four integrated business lines connecting natural gas production through to carbon-neutral energy delivery. Parent company Banpu North America Corporation (Bangkok-based, SET-listed) holds 67.65% of BKV as of January 2026. BKV HQ: Denver, CO.

01
Natural Gas Production
Operated through BKV Upstream Midstream, LLC. Largest Barnett Shale operator with ~563,000 net acres and 835.5 MMcfe/day net production. Key acquisitions: Devon Barnett, Exxon Barnett, Bedrock Production (BKV Barnett II, 96,000 acres, ~1 Tcfe reserves, closed Sept 2025).
Barnett Shale · NE Pennsylvania
02
Natural Gas Midstream
Gathering, compression, processing, and transportation supporting upstream production. Partnership with EnLink Midstream — EnLink processing plants are the CO₂ source for Barnett Zero capture. Operated under BKV Upstream Midstream, LLC.
North Texas Infrastructure
03
Power Generation
BKV-BPP Power JV owns Temple I and Temple II combined-cycle gas-fired plants in Temple, TX (Bell County). 2,246 GWh generated in 2025. BKV increased ownership to 75% in January 2026. Positioned for ERCOT load growth from AI and data center demand.
Temple, TX · ERCOT · 75% BKV
04
CCUS — dCarbon Ventures
Wholly-owned subsidiary driving all CCUS development and operations. Barnett Zero operational. Eagle Ford and Cotton Cove in execution. BKVerde early-stage assets. Funded through BKV-CIP Joint Venture with Copenhagen Infrastructure Partners ($500M committed).
TX · LA · Target: 19 Mtpy CO₂

Business Model

Revenue Model & Commercial Strategy

Five distinct revenue streams, all connected by the closed-loop strategy. The 45Q tax credit is the economic engine that makes CCUS profitable rather than a compliance cost.

⛽
Natural Gas Sales
Core revenue from Barnett Shale and NE Pennsylvania gas production. Top 20 US producer. Hedged commodity exposure. $894M TTM revenue base provides stable cash flow to fund CCUS investment.
Primary Revenue Driver
💰
45Q Tax Credits
Federal credit of $85/ton for CO₂ permanently sequestered in Class VI saline formations. $35/ton for Class II. Inflation Reduction Act extended and expanded the credit through 2033. Central to dCarbon project economics.
$85/ton · Class VI Wells
🍃
Carbon Sequestered Gas (CSG)
Premium natural gas product with Scope 1, 2, and 3 carbon neutrality certification. Differentiated offering for industrial and utility customers seeking low-carbon energy without purchasing offsets.
Premium Product · Growing Demand
🏭
Third-Party CO₂ Sequestration
BKV purchases CO₂ waste streams from third-party gas processors and sequesters for a fee. EnLink and Comstock are current/planned partners. Scales dCarbon revenue beyond BKV's own emissions footprint.
Comstock · EnLink · TBD

Engineering & Project Management

Project Phases & Engineering Scope

CCUS project delivery requires PE and PM capability across all phases of the capital project lifecycle — from early feasibility through commissioning and operations handover. The surface/subsurface interface is a defining coordination challenge at dCarbon.

Note on Select Phase Projects: Based on publicly available information, the Comstock/Haynesville facilities (Bethel and Marquez, East Texas) and the BKVerde Whites Bayou project (Liberty County, TX) are the most likely candidates for what BKV describes internally as projects currently in Select phase — given their announced non-binding agreement status and active EPA permitting activity respectively. This is an informed assumption only. BKV has not publicly confirmed which specific projects are designated Select at any given time.
01
Pre-FEED / Select Phase
Review subsurface injection pressure and rate specifications — translate to surface facility design basis
Develop preliminary PFDs and P&IDs for compression, dehydration, and pipeline systems
Support Class II or Class VI permit application — surface scope and MRV plan coordination
Evaluate site constraints: access, utilities, lay-down area, pipeline routing, easements
Develop preliminary capital cost estimate and project schedule (Class 4/5)
Coordinate with environmental and regulatory consultants on permitting timeline
Support FEL-1 deliverables for FID decision package
02
FEED / Define Phase
Manage engineering firm through FEED deliverables — P&IDs, equipment datasheets, line lists, C&E diagrams
Coordinate HAZOP/HAZID studies and action item closure
Issue long-lead equipment RFPs: compression trains, dehydration units, wellhead systems, controls
Evaluate vendor bids — technical compliance review against data sheet requirements
Develop detailed cost estimate (Class 2/3) and project execution plan
Establish project controls baseline: WBS, P6 schedule, risk register, change control
Coordinate surface/subsurface interface — injection specification compliance verification
Support FID package for BKV/CIP JV investment approval
03
Procurement & Fabrication
Issue purchase orders for long-lead equipment — track delivery milestones and expedite as needed
Review and approve vendor data submittals: drawings, datasheets, QC plans, MCC checklists
Conduct or coordinate factory acceptance tests (FAT) for compression and control packages
Monitor fabrication schedule versus required delivery dates — flag critical path slippage
Manage RFIs and technical queries between engineering and vendors
Track change orders — evaluate cost and schedule impact, obtain approvals
Coordinate transportation and logistics for oversized equipment to remote site locations
04
Construction
Manage EPC contractor or construction manager — daily progress, look-ahead schedules
Conduct field walks — verify construction compliance with drawings and specifications
Manage punch list development and A/B categorization (pre-startup vs. post-startup)
Coordinate inspection activities: welding NDE, pressure testing, instrument loop checks
Track and manage construction RFIs and field change notices
Interface with regulatory inspectors (Railroad Commission, EPA, state agencies)
Maintain cost and schedule variance tracking — weekly project controls reports
Enforce site safety requirements — BKV Zero Harm culture
05
Commissioning & Startup
Develop or review pre-commissioning and commissioning procedures for compression, dehydration, and injection systems
Manage mechanical completion walkdowns and punch list A-item closure before first injection
Maintain contractor accountability during commissioning — schedule pressure on punch list closeout
Coordinate with subsurface team on injection ramp-up protocol and well monitoring requirements
Support MRV plan implementation — regulatory compliance for 45Q credit eligibility
Witness performance testing and document against design basis specifications
Drive turnover package: as-builts, O&M manuals, spare parts, warranties, training
Formal handover to BKV operations team with complete documentation
06
Project Controls & Reporting
Maintain project schedule in P6 or MS Project — update actuals, flag critical path changes
Prepare weekly and monthly project status reports for BKV/dCarbon leadership and JV partners
Track actual vs. budgeted cost — identify variances and develop corrective action plans
Manage risk register — update probability/impact scores, assign mitigations, track actions
Coordinate change control process — scope documentation, cost impact analysis, approval workflow
Support 45Q credit documentation — ensure injection volumes and MRV records are maintained
Coordinate across dCarbon project team daily cadence (8 AM MT team sync)
